## 📌 Introduction

This repository serves as my personal library of security audit reports, reviews, and technical analyses for:

- **DeFi protocols** (AMMs, lending, yield, stablecoins, etc.)  
- **Chain infrastructure** (L1/L2s, bridges, validators, tooling)  
- **Web3 applications** (NFT platforms, wallets, oracles, governance, etc.)  

All artifacts are written from the perspective of a **senior‑level security researcher and protocol‑level engineer**, with emphasis on:

- **Architecture‑level flaws** vs. isolated smart‑contract bugs  
- **Trust assumptions and attack surfaces** across stack layers  
- **Mitigation strategies and hardening recommendations** for teams and auditors 
- **No room for false positives or negatives**

## 🛠️ How to Use This Repository

### For developers and auditors

- Use the reports as **reference material** for understanding:
  - Common attack vectors and misconfigurations in DeFi and chain infra.  
  - How to structure risk findings and mitigation tables.  
  - How to reason about **cross‑layer trust** (e.g., between contracts, oracles, relayers, governance).

- Study the write‑ups as **case‑based learning material** on:
  - Incentive misalignments in DeFi.

  - Interactions between protocol‑level architecture and security.

### For project teams

- Use this repo to:
  - Compare how different protocols tackle similar security challenges.  
  - Validate your own threat‑modeling assumptions and design patterns against past flaws.

## 📄 Style and Content Notes

- Reports are written in **Markdown**, with emphasis on:
  - Clear **risk classification** (e.g., Critical / High / Medium / Low).  
  - Structured **findings sections** and **reproducibility notes** where applicable.  
- Some reports may include **pseudo‑code, diagrams (Mermaid), or simplified math** to explain attack vectors.  
- All content is provided **as‑is**; it is **not legal or financial advice** and should not be treated as a substitute for a formal, scoped audit engagement.
